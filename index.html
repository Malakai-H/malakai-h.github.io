Cloud Types

Cloud Formation

Step-by-Step

  • ONE

    Heat from the Sun evaporates water on the Earth's surface.

  • TWO

    Water enters Earth's atmosphere as water vapor.

  • THREE

    Water vapor cools and condenses onto very small droplets of water and/or ice as it rises.

  • FOUR

    Water droplets stick onto condensation nuclei, solid microscopic particles in the atmosphere, and condenses onto them.

  • FIVE

    Water drops form cloud droplets once condensed, which form clouds when bundled together.

Cumulus Clouds




Name Meaning

Heaped

Weather

Usually predict fair weather on a sunny day

Altitude

Low-Level (1,000-5,000 ft.)

Composition

Water droplets, ice crystals, or a mix of both

Cumulonimbus Clouds




Name Meaning

Heaped Rainstorm

Weather

Extreme Storms

Altitude

Low-Level and vertically develops until High-Level (39,000-69,000 ft.)

Composition

Water droplets and especially ice crystals in its upper portion

Stratus Clouds




Name Meaning

Layer

Weather

Fair but gloomy weather with a chance of light mist or drizzle

Altitude

Low-Level (1,200-7,000 ft.)

Composition

Water droplets or ice crystals
